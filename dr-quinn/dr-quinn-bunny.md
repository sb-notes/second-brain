# Dr Quinn — Bunny

Bunny's health profile and ongoing monitoring. Bunny is a twin born premature at 34 weeks in early 2024, currently around 24 months old. Normal size and height for age. Fair-skinned.

## Background and known history

Bunny spent her first four months in and out of her birth mother's care, with hospital placements during this period. She was then fostered from around four to nineteen months by the same `foster-carers-1` placement that held her twin. The foster home was loving but food-focused, with a diet high in processed food, milk, and snacks. Bunny presented as hypervigilant on placement, which has been understood as attachment-related rather than medical.

Bunny had a hospital stay of two nights with supplemental oxygen treatment for bronchiolitis at some point in her first 18 months — this was declared on the ASQ-3 form by Carciofi but the exact date was not specified. The oxygen requirement indicates a moderate rather than mild episode. This should be added to her formal medical history and captured in any health passport or life-story documentation going forward.

## Current physical health

Overall very healthy with a developing immune system. Eczema mostly resolved; dry skin managed with calendula cream and natural bedding. Walking confidently, running well, mastering the climbing frame including the arch, slide, and Pikler triangle.

## Active monitoring

Loose stools episode through late March 2026. First diarrhoea episode 12 March, attributed to excess berries the previous evening and breakfast plus kefir second-ferment fruit. By 20–22 March, three consecutive days of loose stools without form. No single dietary trigger identified for the second run.

Decisions taken in response: from 12 March, Bunny stopped receiving kefir second-ferment fruit (Fox continues as before). Berries moderated but not eliminated. From 22 March, kefir volume reduced to 1 teaspoon per serving across all three daily drinks (morning milk, lunchtime juice, evening milk). Re-titration plan in place: once stools solid for 5–7 days, increase one serving at a time by 1 teaspoon per week. Bunny's reactive gut means her ceiling will likely sit lower than Fox's long-term — target maintenance around 1–2 teaspoons per serving, once or twice daily, once established.

Breakfast adjusted from 28 March: Bunny on half rice bubbles + corn flakes with half overnight oats (lower fibre than Fox's mix to firm stools).

## LMFM portion observations

One notable episode on 12 April: extremely large dinner (baked beans on toast with lentils and vegetables); stomach visibly distended and uncomfortable after; screaming at Carciofi; in the bath kept saying "poo poo" but unable to pass a bowel movement; went to bed loaded and uncomfortable. The cereal mix lightening (rice bubbles + corn flakes base with smaller amounts of bran flakes) introduced 10 April is helping with portion size and pace.
